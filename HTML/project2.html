THIRD EQUATION OF MOTION

The third equation of motion describes the relationship between an object's final velocity, initial velocity, constant acceleration, and displacement. It is especially useful for solving problems where the time of motion is not known.

The formula is:

v2=u2+2as

Where:
    (v) = final velocity
    (u) = initial velocity
    (a) = constant acceleration
    (s) = displacement (distance covered)

Example applicationImagine a car starting from rest and accelerating at 2 m/s² for a distance of 100 meters. To find its final velocity (\(v\)), you would use the third equation of motion.

Initial velocity (u): The car starts from rest, so \(u=0\) m/s.
    Acceleration (a): \(a=2\) m/s².
    Displacement (s): \(s=100\) m.

By substituting these values into the equation:

       v2=02+2(2)(100)
               v2=400
               v=√400
v=20m/s

The final velocity of the car is 20 m/s.
